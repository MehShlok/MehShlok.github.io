Long-tailed/Out-of-Distribution classification and Anomaly detection

Proposed a unified PAC-learnable anomaly detection framework grounded in measure theory (specifically the Radon–Nikodým derivative), outperforming State-Of-The-Art on 96 real-world datasets.

Conducted a large-scale benchmark of 20+ ML and DL-based anomaly detection methods across various datasets (uni-variate and multivariate, temporal and time-independent, with varying number of anomalies from less than 5% to more than 30%), revealing tree-based methods often outperform deep models in low-data, low-anomaly regimes.
